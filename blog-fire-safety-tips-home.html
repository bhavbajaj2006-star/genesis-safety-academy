10 Fire Safety Tips At Home

Back to Blog
Fire Safety 27 May 2025
  1. Keep smoke alarms in every location — outside bedroom areas, and on every level of your house. Check monthly and replace batteries at least annually.
  2. Learn to operate a fire extinguisher. Use the PASS method: Pull the pin, Aim at the base of the fire, Squeeze the lever, and Sweep left to right. Install fire extinguishers in important zones such as the kitchen and garage.
  3. Keep matches and electronic lighters away from kids — store them on high, locked shelves, and teach children that fire is not a joke.
  4. Operate electronic heaters carefully. Keep space heaters at least 3.3 feet from anything flammable, and never leave them running carelessly.
  5. Be vigilant in the kitchen. Don't leave the kitchen while cooking, especially with high flame, and keep combustible materials like towels and curtains away from burners.
  6. Avoid excess load on electrical sockets. Use one heat-producing appliance per socket, check wires for cracks, and avoid running them under carpets.
  7. Handle wax candles with precaution. Never leave burning candles unattended — use strong holders and keep them away from combustible items.
  8. Store combustible liquids carefully. Keep materials like petrol, paint, varnishes and perfume in authorised containers, away from heat sources.
  9. Wash dryer lint traps periodically. Lint build-up is a dangerous fire risk — clean it after every use and check the vent system regularly.
  10. Prepare a fire evacuation plan. Practise it every six months and make sure everyone in the household knows alternate ways to evacuate every room.

These are the 10 basic home fire safety measures that can help protect your family and property — simple steps that make a big difference in preventing and responding to fires.
